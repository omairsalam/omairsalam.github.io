Having such an immersive experience convinced me that my career truly lies in the tech world.

Research

Computational Nuclear Physics

Jefferson Lab• June 2013 - August 2015

Mentor
Dr Gerrard P. Gilfoyle

Background

One of the fundamental goals of nuclear physics is to understand the structure and behavior of strongly interacting matter in terms of its basic constituents, quarks and gluons. An important step towards this goal is the characterization of the internal structure of the nucleon (a proton or neutron); the elastic electric and magnetic form factors of these sub-atomic particles are key ingredients of this characterization. The elastic electromagnetic form factors describe the charge and current distributions inside the nucleon. These form factors are among the most basic observables of the nucleon.

Research

I worked on the simulating the scattering of neutrons and protons off different targets with the aim of using these scattering patterns to discern qualities of the target. We developed a software called QUEEG - Quasielastic Event Generator which models the internal motion of nucleons in deuterium.
In my second summer of research, I created a Dual Target Simulation (Dueterium and Hydrogen) for an experiment in the Jefferson Lab (a US National Laboratory). My target was added to the official Jefferson Lab Code Repository: Dual Target
