I am in love with Computer Science and everything it has to offer. Having such an immersive experience convinced me that my career truly lies in the tech world.

Work

Nuclear Physics Research

United States Department of Energy • June 2013 - Present

One of the fundamental goals of nuclear physics is to understand the structure and behavior of strongly interacting matter in terms of its basic constituents, quarks and gluons. An important step towards this goal is the characterization of the internal structure of the nucleon (a proton or neutron); the elastic electric and magnetic form factors of these sub-atomic particles are key ingredients of this characterization. The elastic electromagnetic form factors describe the charge and current distributions inside the nucleon. These form factors are among the most basic observables of the nucleon. I work on the simulating the scattering of neuterons and protons off different targets with the aim of using these scattering paterns to discern qualities of the target. We have developed a software called QUEEG - Quasielastic Event Generator which models the internal motion of nucleons in deuterium. The major softwares I used in this research include Geant4 and Geant4 Monte Carlo Simulation, which simulates the the passage of particles through matter and Root, a C++ based data analysis framework.
